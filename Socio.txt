4.1 
For ISD Privacy Framework, consider four dimensions: physical, social, psychological and informational. 
The physical dimension refers to the environment (e.g. workspace, office, etc.) where an individual may seek physical solitude. 
Social privacy refers to the freedom to withdraw from, or enter into, interactions with others.
Psychological privacy is closely related to the social dimension, but refers only to the individual psyche. 
Finally, informational privacy refers to an individual‟s ability to control personal information. 
These dimensions are not unambiguously discrete, and there is some overlap between them.

这一节主要写项目会带来什么隐私风险。比如，用户使用这个机器人，要填写知情同意书，知道这个机器人要收集人脸数据；用户可以随时退出和机器人的交互，即用户可以随时关闭机器人；机器人采用的技术是非侵入式的，不会在不知情的情况下收集数据；机器人可能的输出，比如语音输出，不会对人造成精神创伤；采集的数据都是本地存储且随时可以删除，隐私风险小。
4.2 
GPDR includes the following:
Data Minimization: Organizations should only collect necessary data and must not excessively collect personal information.
Data Subject Rights: Data subjects have the right to access, rectify, erase their personal data, and the right to restrict its processing.
Transparency: Organizations must clearly inform data subjects about the purpose and intended use of their data before collecting it.
Security Safeguards: Organizations must implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data security, preventing unauthorized access and processing.

这一节主要写收集数据方面的问题。比如，只收集必要的数据，不会收集额外的数据即机器人使用中只采集拥有者的面部数据；数据拥有者可以完全控制数据，随时可以删除；不牵扯到任何组织，机器人不会将收集的数据传输出去；采用技术措施对人脸数据进行保护，比如hash。
